# Adversarial Kill Test Protocol — Quiet Thunder

## Overview
5 testers from target audience segments evaluate the product's copy against competitors in blind A/B tests. All materials are fully anonymised — testers see only "Product A/B" or "App A/B/C/D" labels. No brand names appear anywhere.

## Tester Recruitment
Recruit 5 people matching these profiles:
- 1-2 journalists or media professionals
- 1 researcher or academic
- 1 privacy-conscious professional (lawyer, therapist, consultant)
- 1 ADHD power user or high-meeting-volume professional

**Recruitment criteria:**
- Must NOT be current users of any meeting transcription tool (avoids brand recognition bias)
- Must have attended 3+ meetings per week in the past month
- Must be comfortable reading English marketing copy

## Materials
- 4 comparison cards (`test/comparison-cards/card-01.html` through `card-04.html`)
- 2 App Store card sets (`test/appstore-cards/set-01.html` and `set-02.html`)
- 2 ad mockup feeds (`test/ad-mockups/feed-01.html` and `feed-02.html`)

**Note on anonymisation:** The test product's position is randomised across all materials:
- Comparison cards: test product is "A" in cards 1 and 3, "B" in cards 2 and 4
- App Store set 1: test product is "App C" (position 3)
- App Store set 2: test product is "App B" (position 2)
- Ad feed 1: test product is Advertisers 1 and 4
- Ad feed 2: test product is Advertisers 2 and 3

## Protocol

### Setup
1. Open all HTML files in a browser before the session starts
2. Use full-screen or a wide window (1200px+) for comparison cards
3. Have the results template open and ready to fill in

### Execution
1. Show materials one at a time, in the order listed below
2. Ask each question BEFORE revealing which product is which
3. Record responses immediately — exact words where possible
4. Do NOT prime testers with any context about what the test product does
5. Do NOT explain what "on-device" means or why privacy matters
6. If a tester asks for clarification, say: "Just go with your first impression"

### Order of Presentation
1. Comparison Card 1
2. Comparison Card 2
3. Comparison Card 3
4. Comparison Card 4
5. App Store Set 1
6. App Store Set 2
7. Ad Feed 1
8. Ad Feed 2

## Questions

### Comparison Cards (show one card at a time)

**Q1: "Looking at these two products, which one do you think costs more?"**
- Measures: premium perception
- Record: which product label they choose (A or B), plus any reasoning they volunteer
- Ask for all 4 cards

**Q2: "Which of these would you trust with a recording of a confidential client meeting?"**
- Measures: trust / privacy signal
- Record: which product label they choose, plus reasoning
- Ask for all 4 cards

**Q3: "In one sentence, what does Product A do? What about Product B?"**
- Measures: clarity of communication
- Record: their exact words for each product
- Ask for cards 1 and 2 only (to avoid fatigue)

**Q4: "If you could only click one, which would you click?"**
- Measures: curiosity / engagement / click intent
- Record: which product label they choose
- Ask for all 4 cards

### App Store Cards (show one set at a time)

**Q5: "Which of these apps would you download first?"**
- Measures: App Store conversion instinct
- Record: which App label they choose (A, B, C, or D)
- Ask for both sets

**Q6: "Read the first 3 lines of each. Which description makes you most curious?"**
- Measures: description hook strength
- Record: which App label they choose, plus any commentary
- Ask for both sets

### Ad Feed (show one feed at a time)

**Q7: "Scrolling through these, would you stop for any of them? Which one(s)?"**
- Measures: thumb-stopping power
- Record: which Advertiser number(s) they name, whether they say "none"
- Ask for both feeds

**Q8: "Does any of these feel like every other app ad you've seen? Which ones?"**
- Measures: differentiation (inverse — we want testers to NOT flag our ads)
- Record: which Advertiser number(s) they flag as generic
- Ask for both feeds

## Post-Test (optional, after revealing identities)

**Q9: "Now that you know which one is ours — did anything about it surprise you?"**
- Measures: perception gap between blind and informed evaluation
- Only ask after ALL blind questions are complete

## Kill Criteria (NON-NEGOTIABLE)

### Automatic KILL signals
- Loses premium perception test (Q1) in 3+ of 4 cards — KILL that copy direction
- Testers cannot articulate what the product does (Q3) — KILL for clarity failure
- "Feels like every other app ad" (Q8) flagged by 3+ testers — KILL for differentiation failure
- Nobody stops scrolling for any test product ad (Q7) across both feeds — KILL the ad concepts
- Loses trust question (Q2) in 3+ of 4 cards — KILL that copy direction for trust messaging

### Automatic SURVIVE signals
- Wins trust question (Q2) in 3+ of 4 cards — STRONG SIGNAL, keep that copy direction
- 3+ testers pick test product for download (Q5) — App Store copy works
- 3+ testers stop for test product ads (Q7) — Ad concepts have stopping power
- Testers accurately describe what the product does (Q3) without prompting — Clarity confirmed

### Ambiguous results (requires discussion)
- Split 2/3 or 3/2 on any metric — copy needs refinement, not killing
- Testers articulate the product differently from intended positioning — may indicate messaging misalignment rather than failure

## Timing
- Each tester session: 20-30 minutes
- Total: 2-3 hours (can run sessions in parallel)
- Allow 5 minutes between materials for note-taking
- Schedule sessions within a 1-week window to avoid copy iteration between tests

## Session Logistics
- In-person preferred (you can watch their eyes and reactions)
- Video call acceptable if in-person is not possible — share screen, not file
- Do NOT send files to testers in advance
- Do NOT let testers screenshot or save materials
- Record the session (with consent) for qualitative analysis
